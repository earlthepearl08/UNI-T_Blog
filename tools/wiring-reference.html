Circuit Breaker Size Guide (Philippine)

Philippine-market copper conductor sizes (PNS/JIS series — the wire actually sold in PH hardware stores) mapped to breaker size. Ampacity per PEC 2017 Table 3.10.1.16 (THHN/THWN copper, 75°C column, 30°C ambient); breaker per PEC 2.40.6 with the small-conductor cap (PEC 2.40.4(D)).

Wire Size (mm²) Ampacity (Cu, 75°C) Max Breaker (A) Typical PH Use
2.0 mm²20 A15ALighting circuits
3.5 mm²25 A20AGeneral convenience outlets
5.5 mm²35 A30A*Heavy-duty outlets, window-type aircon
8.0 mm²50 A50ASplit-type aircon, electric water heaters
14 mm²65 A60ASub-panel feeders, electric ranges
22 mm²85 A80ASub-panel / small-home main feed
30 mm²100 A100AMain service feed
38 mm²115 A110ALarge main service entrance
50 mm²130 A125ALarge residential / light-commercial main
60 mm²150 A150ACommercial service
80 mm²175 A175ACommercial service entrance

*5.5 mm² carries ~35 A but the breaker is capped at 30 A by the small-conductor rule (PEC 2.40.4(D)). The conductor ampacity — not the breaker — is the design starting point; the breaker must protect the wire. Derate for ambient >30°C and for more than 3 current-carrying conductors in a raceway. For a single motor, size the conductor at ≥125% of motor full-load current (PEC Art. 4.30). Nominal voltages: 230V single-phase; 400V/230V three-phase (legacy 380V/220V).

Pro Tip: Always verify wire gauge with a UNI-T UT61E+ multimeter (continuity and resistance check) before connecting to a breaker. Use a UT210E clamp meter to confirm actual load current.
